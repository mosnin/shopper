## The one-line essence

*In a single sentence, what is this brand at its core? (Jobs's test: if you
can't say it shortly, it isn't clear yet.)*

## Why we exist

*The belief behind the product. Not what it does — what it stands for, and the
dent it intends to make.*

## Who it's for

*The person this is for, described as a human, not a demographic. Who is it
deliberately NOT for?*

## Voice & tone

- **We sound like:** *three to five adjectives + a one-line description*
- **We never sound like:** *the traps to avoid*
- **Vocabulary we use / avoid:** *signature words; banned words*
- **Example — on copy:** *write one real sentence in the brand voice*
- **Example — on an error:** *write the same idea as an error message*

## Values

*Three to five things the brand will not compromise on, each with one line on
what it means in practice.*

## Positioning

- **Category:** *what shelf is this on?*
- **Alternative / status quo:** *what people use today instead*
- **Our wedge:** *the one reason to switch, stated sharply*
- **Positioning statement:** *For [who], [product] is the [category] that [unique
  value], because [reason to believe].*

## Visual identity

- **Logo:** *link / description / usage rules*
- **Color:** *primary, secondary, semantic — with values*
- **Typography:** *typefaces and where each is used*
- **Imagery & iconography:** *style, do's and don'ts*
- **Layout & space:** *density, grid, the role of whitespace*
- **Motion:** *how things move; the personality of transitions*

## The feeling

*Visceral, behavioral, reflective (Norman's three levels): the gut first
impression, the feel of daily use, and the story the user tells themselves after.
Name the emotion you're designing for.*

## Anti-brand

*What would betray this brand? The things that, if we did them, would mean we'd
lost the plot.*
