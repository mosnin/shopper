## Summary

*One paragraph: what this is, who it's for, and the outcome it produces. A new
team member should grasp the whole thing from this alone.*

## Background & problem

*What problem are we solving, for whom, and why now? Link to the value
proposition and any research. (Don't restate the value prop — reference it.)*

## Goals & non-goals

- **Goals:** *the specific outcomes this must achieve, as measurable as possible*
- **Non-goals:** *what this explicitly will NOT do (focus is a requirement)*

## Users & use cases

*The primary user(s) and the concrete scenarios. For each: who, in what context,
trying to do what. Norman's lens: real people on their worst day.*

## Requirements

*State each as a requirement with an owner's name attached (Musk's rule: every
requirement traces to a person who'll defend it — no anonymous "the system must").
Mark priority. Be ruthless: the best requirement is the one you deleted.*

## Experience *(Norman)*

*The key flows at a high level — happy path and the critical unhappy paths
(empty, error, first-run). Link to the user-experience doc for the detailed
journey; don't duplicate it here.*

## Success criteria

- **How we'll know it worked:** *the metrics or observable outcomes*
- **The bar for "great":** *not just "it works" — what makes it insanely great
  here (Jobs's lens)*
- **Definition of done:** *a real person succeeds, unaided, the first time*

## Constraints & dependencies

*Technical, legal, brand, timeline, and external dependencies. Musk's lens: which
of these are physics, and which are just habit we can delete?*

## Open questions & risks

*What's unresolved, what could go wrong, and what we'd need to learn. Flag the
ones that block.*

## Out of scope / later

*Deliberately deferred. Capture it so it's not lost, and so scope stays honest.*
