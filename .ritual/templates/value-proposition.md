# Value Proposition — <!-- PRODUCT NAME -->

> Owned by **Jobs + the banker**. Who it's for, the problem worth solving, why
> us — and whether the value created can sustain the product that creates it.
> Desirability and viability, in one document.

Replace every prompt in *italics*. Be specific; vague value props build vague
products.

---

## The customer

- **Who, exactly:** *the specific person/segment, in their real context*
- **Their goal:** *what they're ultimately trying to achieve (not the feature —
  the outcome)*
- **Today's workaround:** *what they do now, and what it costs them in time,
  money, effort, or feeling*

## The problem

- **The pain:** *the problem, stated from the customer's point of view*
- **Why it's unsolved:** *why existing options fail or fall short*
- **Severity & frequency:** *how much it hurts × how often — is this a vitamin or
  a painkiller?*

## The promise

- **What we do:** *the core value, in one sentence*
- **The before → after:** *their world before us → their world with us*
- **Why us:** *what makes our approach uniquely able to deliver this*
- **Proof:** *why they should believe it (evidence, demo, mechanism)*

## The wedge

*The single sharpest reason to adopt now — the one thing so much better it's worth
switching for. Resist listing ten benefits; find the one.*

## Differentiation

- **Alternatives:** *the real competitors, including "do nothing"*
- **Our edge:** *the difference that matters to the customer (not to us)*
- **What we deliberately don't do:** *focus is a value prop too*

## Economics *(the banker)*

- **How value is captured:** *the revenue model, as part of the product, not
  bolted on*
- **Unit economics, roughly:** *cost to make/serve one vs. lifetime value of one*
- **Recurring vs. one-off:** *where the repeating flow is*
- **The moat:** *what makes success today make success tomorrow easier*
- **Willingness to pay:** *what the value is worth to the customer, and why*

## The one-liner

*Put it together: For [who], who [need/problem], [product] is a [category] that
[key benefit]. Unlike [alternative], we [key differentiator].*
