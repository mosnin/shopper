# THE ALTAR

> The Ritual dreams. The Altar grounds. You bring the Ritual's vision here, lay it
> down, and grind it against reality — first principles, hard constraints, real
> proof — until only what is **true and buildable** remains. Where the Ritual
> escapes the mean, the Altar collapses to the truth.

Read this once. Then perform it on anything the Ritual decided to build but
hasn't yet proven. The Altar is the second of three movements:
**[the Ritual](RITUAL.md) → the Altar → [the Magic](MAGIC.md).**

---

## What the Altar is

The convergent opposite of the Ritual. Same five methods (`methods/README.md`),
but the leader changes and the tiebreaker changes:

| | The Ritual | The Altar |
|---|---|---|
| Question | *Should it exist? Is it great?* | *Is it true? Can it be built?* |
| Motion | Diverge — escape the mean | Reduce — collapse to the truth |
| Starts at | The human, the experience | The physics, the constraint |
| Leads | **Vision** | **The engineer** (with the producer) |
| Tiebreaker | **Taste breaks ties** | **Truth breaks ties** — you cannot out-vote physics |
| The enemy | The obvious, the mean | The assumption, the untested belief, wishful thinking |
| Can return | **KILL** (shouldn't exist) | **FALSIFIED** (reality won — the idea must change) |

The Altar does not care how great the idea is. It cares whether it survives
contact with reality. A beautiful vision that fails on the Altar is not a tragedy
— it's the cheapest save you'll ever get.

---

## The one law

A claim is grounded by **proof, not confidence.** The Altar runs the evidence
ladder *upward* — its whole job is to take a belief and drag it into reality:

```
ASSERTED  →  REASONED  →  TESTED  →  OBSERVED
 (a wish)    (an argument) (it ran)  (reality answered)
```

The Ritual is allowed to leave gates at REASONED and write them down as **debt
owed to reality**. **The Altar exists to pay that debt.** It is forbidden to
declare a thing grounded while it sits on a wish or an argument — it must climb to
TESTED or OBSERVED, or it must report the truth: *this cannot be built as dreamt.*

**FALSIFIED is a win.** When reality refuses the vision, saying so early — before
you've built the whole thing on a false floor — is the most valuable move on the
Altar. Hand it back to the Ritual to change the product. Never fake a rung; an
invented OBSERVED is the one unforgivable act (`framework/05-the-gate-card.md`).

## Perform the Altar — the reduction

Take a Gate Card from the Ritual (its REASONED gates and its *owed-to-reality*
debts are your work list). Then, for each claim that matters, run the reduction.
Full method: [`framework/06-the-altar-loop.md`](framework/06-the-altar-loop.md).

**1 · Lay it down.** Restate the vision as a *falsifiable claim*, not a wish.
   Not "users will love sharing" — "≥40% of first-run users will share within a
   week." If it can't be made false, it can't be grounded.

**2 · Reduce.** Strip the claim to first principles. What do physics, math, and
   the domain *irreducibly require*? Ignore how anyone solved it before.

**3 · Doubt.** Challenge every requirement and assumption. Each must trace to a
   name and a reason, or it's deleted. The best requirement is no requirement.

**4 · Constrain.** Find the one real bottleneck the whole thing waits on.
   Everything else is noise until it's solved. Aim everything there.

**5 · Prove.** Build the *smallest thing that converts belief into evidence* — the
   spike, the experiment, the measurement. Not the product; the proof. Record it
   on a Proof (`templates/proof.md`).

**6 · Discharge.** Climb the Gate Card's rungs with what you proved — REASONED →
   TESTED → OBSERVED — or return **FALSIFIED** with exactly what reality said and
   what must change. Hand the card back.

## The membrane — how the Ritual and the Altar connect

They are not two silos. They share one artifact: the **Gate Card.** The Ritual
writes debts *up* the ladder; the Altar climbs them *down* into reality and writes
the proof back. The card passes between them until every gate that matters stands
on TESTED or OBSERVED — and only then is it ready for the Magic.

A debt with nowhere to go is the failure the Altar exists to end.

---

## How you behave, on the Altar

- **Truth over taste, here.** On the Altar, reality outranks the vision. Report
  what is, not what you wish were so.
- **Prove the smallest thing.** The fastest path to truth is the tiniest
  experiment, not the biggest build. Cycle in hours, not weeks.
- **Delete before you optimize.** Most "hard problems" are requirements that
  shouldn't exist. Question them first.
- **Falsify gladly.** Hunt for the test that could kill the idea. Finding it early
  is the win.
- **Honest rungs only.** Name the rung you actually reached. Evidence you can't
  gather is *owed to reality* and handed to the founder, never invented.
