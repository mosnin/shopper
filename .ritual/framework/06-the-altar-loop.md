# Framework 06 — The Altar Loop (the reduction)

> The deep method for [`ALTAR.md`](../ALTAR.md). Where the development loop
> (Framework 04) is the Ritual's divergent build cycle, this is the Altar's
> convergent grounding cycle — the machine that turns a *belief* into *evidence*
> and pays down the debts a Gate Card owes to reality.

The Ritual is allowed to decide on reasoned judgment and write "owed to reality"
on the gates it couldn't yet prove. That debt is not a flaw — it's honest. But it
must be paid before scale, and the Altar is where you pay it. The currency is
proof; the enemy is the untested assumption.

---

## When you come to the Altar

You arrive holding a **Gate Card** from the Ritual. Your work list is everything
on it that stands on a wish or an argument:

- Any gate at **ASSERTED** (never allowed to ship) or **REASONED** (debt).
- Everything in the card's **owed to reality** field.
- Any claim whose failure would collapse the product.

Rank them by *what would hurt most if it's false.* Ground the load-bearing
beliefs first; a true thing built on a false floor still falls.

---

## The reduction — six moves

### 1 · Lay it down — make it falsifiable
Restate the vision as a claim that reality could *prove false.* "Users will love
it" cannot be falsified, so it cannot be grounded. "≥40% of first-run users share
within 7 days" can. If you can't state the test that would kill it, you don't yet
understand the claim. This is the Altar's first discipline: no wishes, only
claims.

### 2 · Reduce — to first principles
Strip the claim to what physics, math, cost, and the domain *irreducibly require*.
Ignore precedent and analogy. Ask: what is actually true here, beneath what
everyone assumes? The answer is usually smaller and stranger than the inherited
version.

### 3 · Doubt — challenge every requirement
Every requirement carries a *name* — a person, not "the system." If you can't
trace it to a human who'll defend it, it's probably wrong. Distrust requirements
from smart people most; you under-question them. Delete before you optimize: the
cheapest, fastest, most reliable part is the one that isn't there.

### 4 · Constrain — find the one true bottleneck
There is always a single constraint the whole thing waits on — the access-control
model, the latency floor, the unit cost, the data you don't have. Name it.
Subordinate everything else to it. Grounding the wrong thing first is wasted
motion.

### 5 · Prove — build the smallest evidence
Now build — but build the *proof*, not the product. The smallest spike, the
narrowest experiment, the one measurement that converts belief into evidence.
What's the fastest thing that could move this claim from REASONED to TESTED? From
TESTED to OBSERVED? Cycle in hours. A high early failure rate is the cost of a
steep learning curve — if nothing's failing, you're not testing the real claim.

### 6 · Discharge — pay the debt or falsify
Take what you proved back to the Gate Card:

- **Climbed:** raise the gate's rung (REASONED → TESTED → OBSERVED) and cite the
  Proof. The debt is paid.
- **Falsified:** the claim met reality and lost. Mark the gate **FALSIFIED**, say
  exactly what reality said, and hand it back to the Ritual — the product must
  change. This is a win, not a loss: you saved everything downstream.

Never leave a load-bearing gate on a wish. Never invent a rung you didn't reach.

---

## FALSIFIED — the Altar's veto

The Ritual can return KILL (*this shouldn't exist*). The Altar's mirror is
**FALSIFIED** (*this can't be built or grounded as dreamt*). It is not failure —
it is the Altar doing its only job. The cost of a falsified claim caught on the
Altar is one experiment. The cost of the same claim caught after launch is the
company. Falsify early, loudly, and gladly.

When you falsify, you don't just say no — you hand back *what reality requires*,
so the Ritual can re-dream within the real constraints. Truth doesn't kill the
vision; it tells the vision where the walls are.

---

## What leaves the Altar

A Gate Card whose load-bearing gates now stand on **TESTED or OBSERVED**, each
with a Proof behind it — or an honest **FALSIFIED** sent back upstream. Either
way, the wishes are gone. What remains is true.

Then, and only then, it goes to the **Magic** ([`../MAGIC.md`](../MAGIC.md)) — to
ship the smallest whole and observe whether the spark is real.

---

## Done when

- Every load-bearing claim is stated as something reality could falsify.
- Each is reduced to first principles, its requirements doubted and trimmed, its
  one constraint named.
- Each is proven by the smallest possible experiment, recorded on a Proof
  (`../templates/proof.md`).
- The Gate Card's debts are discharged to TESTED/OBSERVED, or returned FALSIFIED
  with what reality requires.
- No load-bearing gate still stands on a wish, and no rung is faked.
