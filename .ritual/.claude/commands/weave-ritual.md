Perform a **Weave Ritual**: $ARGUMENTS

Take the candidate takes (from the just-run Simulation Ritual, or the set named
above) and produce **one** decision. This is **not** "pick the best one." Full
method: `framework/10-the-simulation.md` + the gates in `framework/05`.

## 1 · Mine each take for its strongest part
For every candidate, name its single best element — and keep **provenance** (which
mind it came from). The minimalist's restraint, the maximalist's signature move,
the engineer's constraint-insight, the distant-field's structural steal.

## 2 · Reconcile through the four gates
Run the strongest parts *together* through **desirable → feasible → deliverable →
viable**. Where two parts conflict, hold the tension and let **vision break the
tie**. Cut any part that fails a gate — even a beautiful one — and say why.

## 3 · Synthesize ONE coherent thing
The output must be a single, whole design — not a Frankenstein of bolted-on
pieces. If the best parts don't cohere, pick the spine that does and graft only
what fits. Coherence over completeness.

## 4 · Red-team, then stamp the card
Run the adversarial pass (`framework/05`): which borrowed part is inflated? what
did *every* mind miss? Then stamp a **Gate Card** (`templates/gate-card.md`) with:
- honest evidence rungs per gate,
- a **`WOVEN FROM:`** line listing which element came from which simulated mind,
- the verdict and any debts owed to reality.

## 5 · Founder Call, then implement
The woven direction is a taste call — surface it to the founder in one glance and
get the nod. Then **implement it** (the post-decision build / the Magic). Record
the decision in the ledger and update the Heading.

End with the woven decision stated plainly, the provenance, and the next step.
