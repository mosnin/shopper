# THE MAGIC

> The Ritual made it *desirable*. The Altar made it *true*. The Magic is where
> they fuse and the thing **comes alive** — a product a real person falls for in
> the first five seconds. The Magic is not declared. It is *earned*, and then
> *observed*.

The third and final movement: **[the Ritual](RITUAL.md) →
[the Altar](ALTAR.md) → the Magic.** This one is short on purpose — because the
Magic is mostly the discipline of *not faking it*.

---

## What the Magic is

The synthesis. When a *proven-desirable* thing (the Ritual) meets a
*proven-buildable* thing (the Altar) and the result is **more than the sum** —
the moment of "it just works," the thing you can't put down. That emergent spark
is the whole point of everything upstream.

The Magic is the **OBSERVED rung made flesh.** Every gate, every proof, every
debt discharged exists to earn this one moment and then *check that it actually
happened.*

---

## The two ways there is no Magic

Most things that ship are missing one half. Name them honestly:

- **FLAT** — feasible and true, but no one's heart moves. It works; nobody cares.
  *(Passed the Altar, failed the Ritual.)*
- **MIRAGE** — everyone wants it, but it can't be built or grounded as dreamt. A
  beautiful promise with no floor. *(Passed the Ritual, failed the Altar.)*

The Magic is the **AND** — desirable *and* true *and* the spark is real. Two out
of three is not "almost magic." It's FLAT or a MIRAGE wearing a launch.

---

## The one law: earn it, then observe it

You may **never declare Magic.** You may only *observe* it. The bar:

1. The Ritual's **Desirable** gate stands at **OBSERVED** — a real person, not a
   guess.
2. The Altar has discharged the **Feasible** and **Deliverable** debts to
   **TESTED/OBSERVED** — it's real, and it reproduces.
3. The **five-second gate** passes *in reality* — you put the smallest whole in
   front of someone who isn't you, and watched the first five seconds land.

Only when all three are true may a Gate Card be stamped **MAGIC — observed.**
Until then you do not have magic. You have a plan, and a plan is owed to reality.

---

## Perform the Magic

1. **Ship the smallest whole.** Not a fraction of a great thing — a small,
   complete, great thing. (The Ritual designed it; the Altar proved it; now make
   it real and seamless, end to end.)
2. **Put it in front of a real person.** The one who isn't you, on their worst
   day. Say nothing. Watch.
3. **Observe the five seconds.** Did their heart move before they understood the
   features? That's the only question. Record what actually happened.
4. **Stamp the verdict.** **MAGIC** (the spark is observed) · **FLAT** (works, no
   spark → back to the Ritual for taste) · **MIRAGE** (loved, can't ground → back
   to the Altar for truth).
5. **Then compound.** A confirmed spark becomes a pattern in
   `docs/decisions/README.md` — what actually made hearts move, promoted to a
   default.

---

## How you behave, in the Magic

- **The spark is observed, never asserted.** "I think they'll love it" is not
  Magic. A watched first-run is.
- **Reality is the only judge.** Not the team, not the demo, not the deck. The
  person in front of it.
- **Two-thirds is a failure, named honestly.** FLAT and MIRAGE are not "close."
  Send them back to the movement that owns the missing half.
- **Whole, not fragment.** Magic lives in the seamless whole. A great part inside
  a broken experience has no spark.

---

*Desirable, true, and alive — observed, not declared. That is the Magic, and it
is the only thing worth shipping.*
